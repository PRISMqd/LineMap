The problem

IV line safety lives in workarounds

At the bedside today, compatibility logic, lumen capacity, and line risk all live in nurses’ heads, external reference sites, and improvised notes. In a crisis, teams burn time logging into the EHR, drilling down to the MAR, and running one‑to‑one checks across dozens of combinations – while a blown line or unstable patient cannot wait.

  • No single source of truth for vascular access – clinicians mentally track where each infusion is running and why.
  • Compatibility logic lives in external reference sites, partial memory, and hurried phone calls, not in the workflow itself.
  • EHRs capture orders, but not actual access topology or lumen assignments – there is no map of “what is running where.”
  • In an emergency, nurses can spend minutes navigating and cross‑checking instead of immediately routing safe, compatible therapy.
Direct‑observation research has found that a meaningful proportion of IV doses involve at least one error in preparation or administration, and several meta‑analyses estimate combined IV preparation/administration error prevalence above 40% in some settings. At the same time, multiple studies show that only a minority of medication errors are ever formally reported, meaning the visible data represent only a slice of the actual burden on patients, clinicians, and systems.

Pssst… the scary part isn’t just the 10–40% error rates in observed IV administrations – it’s how little of the real picture those observations touch. Only a thin slice of doses are ever directly watched, and even fewer events make it into incident reports. If a big chunk of that tiny visible slice is problematic, the true volume of near misses, delayed meds, and quiet failures to rescue is far bigger than any dashboard is showing you right now.

The solution

Structured intelligence for IV access

LineMap gives clinicians a live, shared map of each patient’s vascular access, infusions, and constraints – so compatibility logic, lumen capacity, and risk signals are handled by the system, not left to memory and multi‑tab workarounds.

Vascular access, mapped

Nurses document line placement directly on an anatomical figure, preserving spatial context and making the interface feel like the patient, not a form.

LumenLogic™ engine

Infusions are assigned to lumens based on compatibility, vesicant status, priority, and line type – reducing cognitive load and catching conflicts before they reach the patient.

Unit‑level situational awareness

Charge nurses see a live board of every patient’s access status, flags, and acuity – supporting staffing, escalation decisions, and rapid reassignment in high‑pressure moments.

Built for emergencies

Quick‑check mode lets clinicians enter all active medications and generate a minimum‑lumen map in seconds – built for blown PIVs, rapid reassignment, and code‑level events.

How it works

From blown line to safe routing in one workflow

LineMap is designed as a point‑of‑care overlay that works alongside your existing EHR. Clinicians interact with a mapped view of vascular access while the engine handles compatibility, capacity, and routing behind the scenes.

1

Capture the access reality

Tap insertion sites on the body figure to document line type, lumen count, and key attributes like central vs. peripheral, dedicated lumens, and vesicant‑only access.

2

Map the infusions

Enter active medications; LumenLogic™ recommends optimal lumen assignments based on compatibility data, vesicant status, and clinical priority – flagging conflicts and rate thresholds in real time.

3

Use quick‑check in crisis

In emergency mode, clinicians can enter all meds that need a home, then drag‑and‑drop to finalize a minimum‑lumen plan – so they can move from blown line to safe, compatible routing without leaving the bedside.
