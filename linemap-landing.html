The Problem

The system
didn't fail.
It never existed.

Nurses managing complex patients juggle multiple lines, lumens, and infusions under pressure — without any intelligent decision support. The result is preventable harm, escalating costs, and clinician burnout.

⚠

Incompatible Infusion Assignment

Drug-drug incompatibility errors occur when lumens are assigned without structured guidance, leading to adverse reactions and line failures.

🔀

No Shared Mental Model

Shift handoffs depend on verbal communication — no single source of truth for active lines and their configurations across the care team.

📋

EHR Blind Spots

EHR systems capture orders but not vascular access topology. There is no map of what is running where — and why.

🧠

Compatibility Logic Carried in Memory

Who's managing the compatibility logic? Right now: the nurse. Alone. Under pressure. Without a system. LineMap changes that.

The Solution

Compatibility. Capacity. In real time.

LineMap gives every clinician a live, structured map of their patient's vascular access — with rules-based lumen assignment, compatibility checking, and safety alerts built in.

01

Body-Map Interaction Model

Clinicians document line placement on an anatomical figure — making the interface feel like the patient, not a form. Spatial context is preserved across the care team.

02

Rules-Based Lumen Assignment

LumenLogic™ assigns infusions to lumens based on compatibility, priority, and line type — reducing cognitive load and preventing assignment errors before they occur.

03

Real-Time Safety Alerts

Incompatibility conflicts, rate threshold breaches, and line age alerts surface automatically — with OMO trauma-informed language for high-stress environments.

04

Patient Access Board

A live unit-level view of every patient's access status — acuity tags, vesicant flags, capacity indicators — giving charge nurses a real-time operational picture.

05

Quick Check · Lumen Planner

Emergency triage mode: enter all active medications and get a minimum lumen map instantly. Built for blown PIVs, rapid reassignment, and high-acuity moments.

06

EHR-Agnostic Architecture

Deployable as a point-of-care overlay without requiring a full EHR replacement. HL7 FHIR compatible. Built to integrate with the systems already in place.

How It Works

A system that keeps up
with them.

1

View the Patient Access Board

Open to a live unit view. Every patient's line count, active infusions, acuity status, and flags — all visible at a glance. No digging. No guessing.

2

Select a Patient · Open the Body Map

Tap the anatomical figure to mark insertion sites. LineMap captures location, line type, and lumen count — a live spatial record of every access point.

3

Run LumenLogic™ Assignment

Enter active medications. The engine recommends optimal lumen routing based on compatibility rules, vesicant status, and clinical priority.

4

Quick Check for Urgent Situations

Blown PIV? Rapid reassignment needed? Enter all meds and get the minimum lumen map in seconds. Drag chips to rearrange.
